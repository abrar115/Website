Chapter 1 - Relations & Functions


Summary: Introduces sets, Cartesian products, relations, types of functions (one-one, onto, bijective), composition of functions, invertible functions, and binary operations. It lays the foundation for algebraic structures.

Chapter 2 - Inverse Trigonometric Functions


Summary: Introduces the concept of inverse trigonometric functions, their domains, ranges, and principal value branches. It covers their graphs and various properties used in solving trigonometric equations and simplifying expressions.

Chapter 3 - Matrices


Summary: Covers the fundamental concepts of matrices, including their types, operations (addition, subtraction, multiplication), and properties of matrix algebra. It also discusses transpose, symmetric, skew-symmetric matrices, elementary row/column operations, and the inverse of a matrix.

Chapter 4 - Determinants


Summary: Focuses on determinants, which are scalar values associated with square matrices. It covers minors, cofactors, properties of determinants, and their applications in finding the area of a triangle, adjoint and inverse of a matrix, and solving systems of linear equations using Cramer's rule.

Chapter 5 - Continuity & Differentiability


Summary: Explores the concepts of continuity of functions at a point and in an interval, and differentiability. It introduces various rules of differentiation, including the chain rule, implicit differentiation, and differentiation of inverse trigonometric and exponential/logarithmic functions, along with Rolle's Theorem and Mean Value Theorem.

Chapter 6 - Applications of Derivatives


Summary: Applies derivatives to various real-world problems. Key topics include rate of change of quantities, finding equations of tangents and normals to curves, identifying increasing and decreasing functions, and determining local maxima and minima using first and second derivative tests.

Chapter 7 - Integrals


Summary: Introduces the concept of integration as the inverse process of differentiation. It covers indefinite and definite integrals, various methods of integration (substitution, integration by parts, partial fractions), and properties of definite integrals.

Chapter 8 - Applications of Integrals


Summary: Focuses on applying definite integrals to calculate the area of regions bounded by curves, lines, and axes. It includes finding the area under simple curves, areas between two curves, and applications in real-world scenarios.
